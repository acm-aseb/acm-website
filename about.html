What is ACM?

The Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) is the world’s largest and most influential professional organization for computing. With a global network of researchers, educators, and practitioners, ACM advances computing as a science and a profession.

ACM plays a vital role in shaping the future of technology by promoting innovation, research, ethical computing, and professional excellence across disciplines such as artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, data science, software engineering, and human–computer interaction.

ACM Student Chapters

ACM Student Chapters act as a bridge between academic learning and the professional computing world. These chapters empower students by providing opportunities to collaborate, lead, innovate, and grow beyond classrooms.

Through student chapters, members gain exposure to real-world challenges, cutting-edge technologies, peer learning, and industry-aligned activities.
